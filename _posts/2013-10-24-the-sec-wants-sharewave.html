Yesterday, the SEC voted unanimously to propose rules for equity crowdfunding. In the statements leading up to the vote, Commissioner Stein cited the importance of cost-effective recordkeeping solutions for issuers offering and selling securities through crowdfunding:

“A business simply must be able to track who its owners are. While I understand that requiring a registered transfer agent would increase the costs to an issuer, I would like to hear from commenters about possible third-party, cost-effective solutions to help crowdfunding issuers manage their recordkeeping responsibilities.” — SEC Commissioner Kara M. Stein, October 23, 2013

We clearly agree. Not only as a method for fraud prevention, but as ardent supporters of greater information transparency for all parties. That’s why Sharewave exists, to help private companies easily and cost-effectively maintain their shareholder records, transfer journals, employee options and company documents.
